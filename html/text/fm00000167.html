Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Florian Matei-Popescu

Description

Object type: Stele
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: No
Provenance: Troesmis
Findspot: in one of the two late Roman forts from Troesmis, in the western one, Romania
Date: In the first half of the second century AD, when the V Macedonica legion was at Troesmis

Bibliography:

  • Gr. Tocilescu, AEM 6, 1882, p. 40, no. 82
  • CIL III 7500
  • ISM V 178

Text

D(is) M(anibus)
T(iberius) Clau(dius)
Ti(berii) filius
Quirina Pris-
5cus Hemesa
ex c(ustode) a(rmorum) vixit
annis L, mil(itavit)
n(is) XXVI
post missio(nem)
10vixit n(os) II
convete-
rani qui et
heredes
b(ene)m(erenti) p(osuerunt)

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

See this file in GIT.



Translation:

To the Manes Gods! In the memory of Tiberius Claudius, the son of Tiberius, from Quirina tribe, Priscus, originated from Hemesa (Macedonia province); former arsenal keeper, he lived 50 years and he served in the army 26 years; after his release from duty he lived two years. His fellow veterans and heirs raised the monument to the well-deserving (comrade).
