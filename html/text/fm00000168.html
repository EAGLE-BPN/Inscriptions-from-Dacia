Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Florian Matei-Popescu

Description

Object type: Stele
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: No 4.5
Provenance: Troesmis
Findspot: in one of the two late Roman forts from Troesmis, in the western one, Romania
Date: In the first half of the second century, when V Macedonica legion was at Troesmis

Bibliography:

  • Gr. Tocilescu, AEM 6, 1882, p. 41, no. 84
  • AEM 11, 1887, p. 31, no. 28
  • CIL III 7502
  • ISM V 186

Text

D(is) M(anibus)
Iul(ius) Ponticus vixit
n(is) XXXII milit(avit)
n(is) XIV in leg(ione) V
5Mac(edonica) natus Amas-
tris, Iul(ius) Ponticus
frater, Sentius Pon-
ticus vet(eranus), Sentius Pon-
ticus mil(es) leg(ionis) V Mac(edonicae)
10fratres cohered(es)
b(ene) m(erenti) d(e) s(uo) p(osuerunt)

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

To the Manes Gods! Iulius Ponticus, who lived 32 years and served in the Fifth Macedonian legion 14 years, was born in Amastris (Pontus et Bithynia province, Paphlagonia region). His "brothers" and heirs raised the monument to the well-deserving on their own expenses: Iulius Ponticus, brother; Sentius Ponticus, veteran; Sentius Ponticus soldier of the Fifth Macedonian legion.
