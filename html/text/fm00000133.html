Text

Dis M(anibus)
Basso patri
et Tsinnae fr(atri)
Zura et Tsiru
5posuerunt
vix(it) an(nis) LXX, Ts(inna)
vix(it) an(nis) XV et Marti(ae) con(iugi)
v(otum) (solvit) l(ibens) m(erito) (sic!)


File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

See this file in GIT.



Translation:

To the Manes Gods! Zura and Tsiru erected the monument for our father Bassus and our brother Tsinna, who lived 70 years and Ts(inna) 15 years and for Martia, the wife of...? Willingly and deservedly (fulfilling) their pledge


Commentary:

The final part not easy to understand, it is not clear whose wife was Martia and why they end the inscription with a formula which was typical for the votive inscription. This is nevertheless an wonderful proof of the reception of the Roman customs by the natives in the province. They just copied that they had seen on other inscriptions, without a proper understanding of the meaning. A curiosity is also the disposing of the DIS M formula, which could be seen as hybrid between DIM formula and DM formula. All the native names are of daco-moesian origin. Very interesting after having a Roman name, the father choose to give native names to his children.
