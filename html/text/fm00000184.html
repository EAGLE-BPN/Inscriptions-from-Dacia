Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Florian Matei-Popescu

Description

Object type: Stele
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: yes 5.5
Provenance: Oescus
Findspot: In the Sucidava Late Roman fortification, Bulgaria
Date: Second half of the first century AD, when V Macedonica legion was garrisoned in the Oescus fortress. Other criteria: the name of the deceased in nominative case; the missing of Dis Manibus formula; the presence of hic situs est formula. All these data speak for an early dating.

Bibliography:

  • Gr. Tocilescu, Fouilles et recherches, Buchares, 1901, p. 190
  • CIL III 14492
  • Gr. Florescu, MonFun Dacia inferior, Bucharest, 1942, p. 29
  • IDR II 203

Text

In the upper register, near the depiction of the soldier, holding a signum : m(ilitavit) a(nnos) XX
Q(uintus) Philippicus
Q(uinti) f(ilius) Mae(cia tribu) Edessa
signi[f]er leg(ionis) V (Macedonicae
5vixit annos XXXX.
H(ic) s(itus) e(st), ex tes(stamento) f(acere) ius(sit)
her(es) [f(aciendum)] c(uravit) arbitr(atu)
Antoni architect(i)
et T[i]ti coriari.

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

See this file in GIT.



Translation:

He served for 20 years. Here lies Quintus Philippicus, the son of Quintus, belonging to the Maecia tribe, from Edessa (Macedonia province), standard's bearer of the fifth (Macedonica ?) legion, lived for 40 years. The heir managed to be done, after the advises of Antonius, the architect, and Titus, the leather worker.


Commentary:

The funerary stele was reused in the late Roman fortification from Sucidava. It belongs, without any doubts, to Oescus, Bulgaria. Therefore, any attempt to link this inscription with the history of the Dacia inferior province is wrong. He originated from Edessa, Macedonia province, since he was member of the Maecia tribe. He served in the fifth legion. If indeed the stele comes from Oescus, than it would have been without any hesitation the V Macedonica legion. Nevertheless, in the area of Lower Danube it is also the V Alaudae only by literary sources attested.
