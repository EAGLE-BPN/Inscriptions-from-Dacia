Translation:

To the Manes Gods, Marcus Cocceius Vitlus, veteran of the Ubii cohort, standard-bearer, who lived 70 years and served 28 years. His wife Claudia, together with his sons, Cocceius Veturius and Cocceius Nardus, all being his heirs, took care to erect the monument. Marcus Cocceius Titio (?) made for the most pious parent.


Commentary:

The last two lines were later added into a very bad manner. The relationship between The veteran was released from duty during Nerva's short reigns in AD 97, after 28 years of military service. Being recruited in his 18 or 20, he was 46 or 48 years old in 97. Since he lived 70 years old, he died around AD 119-122, in Hadrian's first years of reign.
