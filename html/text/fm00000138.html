Text

Impera[n-]
te domin[o n(ostro)]
Severo [Ale-]
x[an]dro
5c(onsule) III et Cas(sio)
Dione II (consule)
Iul(ius) Vit[ales]
(centurio) leg(ionis) ex [pr(a)ec(epto)]
v(iri) c(larissimi) Mant[enni]
10co(n)s(ularis) n(ostri) t[ermi(nos)]
fix(it)

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

While our Lord Severus Alexander was Emperor and consul for the third time, together with Cassius Dio for the second time, Iulius Vitales, centurion of the legion, set the limits according to the decision reached by the highly regarded man, our governor, Mantennius (Sabinus).


Commentary:

See also ISM V 57 a, b, discovered at Ulmetum, whit the same text. The second consul is the famous historian Cassius Dio.
