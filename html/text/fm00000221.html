Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Florian Matei-Popescu

Description

Object type: Stele
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: yes - hederae distinguentes 5.8
Provenance: vicus Quintionis / vicus Celerius
Findspot: Sinoe / Casapchioi and Vadu / Caraharman, Romania
Date: Second half of the second century, first half of the third century AD.

Bibliography:

  • Gr. Tocilescu, Fouilles et recherches, p. 207, no. 32
  • CIL III 7525 + 14449 = ISM I 339

Text

D(is) M(anibus)
T(itus) Manius Bassi-
anus vixit an-
n(is) LXII, bul(euta) His(triae)
5Manius Vitales, ne-
pos eius, vixit an-
ni(s) VIIII. [Vitalia-]
nus Bassianus
fil(ius) eius et Scapul-
10lia Gemella coniunx
eius s(ibi) et et sibi (sic!) paren-
tibus benemerentis
posuerunt
Have viator.

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

See this file in GIT.



Translation:

To the Manes Gods! Titus Manius Bassianus, who lived 62 years, member of the Histrian Council; Manius Vitales, his grandson, who lived 9 years. Vitalianus Bassianus, his son, and Scapullia Gemella, his wife, set for them and for their well deserving parents! Fare-thee-well, passer-by!


Commentary:

Two conjoining fragments of a stele.
