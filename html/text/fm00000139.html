Description

Object type: Altar
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Votive
Letters: No 3.5
Provenance: Capidava
Findspot: Reused in the Late Roman fort, Romania
Date: 138 - 161 AD, dedication for the health of Antoninus Pius and Verus Aurelius Caesar (the future Marcus Aurelius)

Bibliography:

  • AE 1934, 102
  • Gr. Florescu, Capidava, p. 86-87
  • ISM V 13

Text

I(ovi) O(ptimo) M(aximo) Tona(n)t[i]
et Iunoni Reginae
et pro [s]al[ut]e Imp(eratoris) T(iti) Ael(ii)
Antonino (sic!) Aug(usti) Pii et Veri Aurelii Caesare (sic!)
5posuit Aelius Cara-

Translation:

To the Thundering Jupiter, the Good and the Greatest, and to Iuno the Queen and for the health of the Emperor Titus Aelius Antoninus Augustus Pius and of the Caesar Verus Aurelius, raised by Aelius Cara[...].
