Text

D(is) M(anibus)
Aur(elius) Hermes paganus
vixit annis LXX, dies XI et
Aur(elia) Meliti coniux eius
5dem vixit annis LX,
menses duobus, dies VII
qui reliquerunt here-
des bonos filios super-
stantis VII, sex quidem
10filios et unam filia[m]
benemerentibus p[i-]
entissimi nati mem[o-]
riam posuerunt
ave vale viator!

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

To the Manes. Aurelius Hermes, who lived in a pagus for 70 years and 11 days, together with his wife, Aurelia Meliti, who lived 60 years, 2 months and seven days. They left good as heirs good children, seven have survived, six sons and one daughter. The most pious children made the grave and raised the monument. Fare-thee-well, passer-by.


Commentary:

paganus - the person who lives in a pagus
