Translation:

[- - -] Titus Valerius, the sone of Titus, from the Pollia tribe, Marcianus, born in the canabae of the legion, veteran of the Fifth Macedonian legion, former beneficiarius of the governor, he began his career in the legion while Emperor Antoninus (Pius) was for consul for the fourth time (145 AD), he served in the Eastern military expedition under the highly regarded men, Statius Priscus, Iulius Severus and Martius Verus (162-166 AD); he served also in the Germanic military expedition under the highly regarded men, Calpurnius Agricola and Claudius Fronto (166-169 AD); he was thereafter honorably released from duty when the legion was in Dacia, in the year of the consuls Cethegus and Clarus (170 AD), under the highly regarded ma Cornelius Clemens; he came back at his home gods; he dedicated this together with Marcia Basilissa, his wife, the priestess (in the text "mother") of the club of the dendrophori (worshipers of Cybele) and with Valeria Longa, his sister, for his and for his fellows good health.
