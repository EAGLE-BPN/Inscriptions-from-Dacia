Text

Mineruae
Aug(ustae)
Ulp(ius) Domit(ius) Her-
mes Aug(ustalis) col(oniae) orna-
5tus ornam(entis) decur(ionalibus)
t(estamento) p(oni) i(ussit)
Val(erius) Threptus et Domitii
Regulus Hipponic(us)
Hermes Onesimus
10Aug(ustales) col(oniae) h(eredes) p(onendum) c(urauerunt) l(oco) d(ato) d(ecurionum) d(ecreto).

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

To Minerva Augusta. Domitius Hermes, augustal of the colonia, granted the honorifc decurion's ornaments, ordered by will for(the statue) to be erected. Valerius Threptus and the Domitii Regulus, Hipponicus, Hermes, Onesimus, augustales of the colonia, took care of it as his heirs. The place was given by a decree of the decurions.
