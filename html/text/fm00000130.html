Description

Object type: Stele
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: No 5-6
Provenance: Capidava
Findspot: Reused in the Late Roman fort, Romania
Date: Second half of the second century AD

Bibliography:

  • ISM V 35

Text

D(is) M(anibus)
Ave domina Ve-
turia vixit n(is)
XXIIII, mens(ibus) II. M(arcus) Ulpi-
5us Piso Veturiae
[uxori sibi-]
que vivo et Veturi(a)e Ulpi-
(a)e et M(arco) Ulpio fili(i)-
s caris(s)imis vivis
10posuit

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

To the Manes Gods! Hail you (be praised) Veturia domina, who lived 24 years and two months. Marcus Ulpius Piso erected the monument for his wife Veturia for himself and for Veturia Ulpia and Marcus Ulpius, the most beloved children, while they were alive.


Commentary:

Two conjunctives parts of a funerary stele. The upper part is preserved in the Constanta Museum, whilst the lower part is to be seen in the courtyard of the site Museum of Capidava.
