Translation:

Claudius Iulian, soldier in the 13th Gemina legion, in the centuria of Claudius Marius, bought a woman slave named Theudotem or otherwise, born in Crete, for the price of 420 denarii, from Claudius Philetus. Alexander son of Antipater was witness. The woman was in good health. If anyone else would want to buy her, he would have to pay the same amount of money to Claudius Iulianus. Claudius Philetus received his money. Sealed in the canabae of the 13th Gemina legion, on the 4th of October, when Bradua and Varus were consuls. Witnesses: Valerius Valens, soldier in the 13th Gemina legion, Cnaeus Valerius Atta, Aelius Dionysos, veteran, Paulinus S..., Iulius Victorinus, Alexander son of Antipater, Claudius Philetus, the seller.
