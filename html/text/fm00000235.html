Indici
Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Florian Matei-Popescu

Description

Object type: Sarcophagus
Material: Limestone
Type of Inscription: Funerary
Letters: Yes 4-5
Provenance: Noviodunum
Findspot: In the ancient necropolis, in the place called "at the two mounds", Romania
Date: Second half of the second century AD

Bibliography:

  • G. Simion, Dacia, NS 38-39, 1994-1995, p. 123
  • Fl. Matei-Popescu, The Roman Army in Moesia inferior, p. 255.

Text

D(is) M(anibus)
Ulpia Iulia vix(it)
an(nis) XL
P(ublius) Aelius Mithres
5ark(arius) clas(is) coniug(i)
b(ene) m(erenti) f(aciendum) c(uravit).

File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

See this file in GIT.



Translation:

To the Gods of the Underworld! Ulpia Iulia who lived forty years. Publius Aelius Mithres the money keeper of the fleet (classis Flavia Moesica) he took care to be done for his well-deserving wife.
