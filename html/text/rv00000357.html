File sorgente prodotto da: EAGLE - Europeana Network of Ancient Greek and Latin Epigraphy

Translation:

When Macrinus and Census were consuls, on the 20th of May, I Flavius Secundinus wrote at the request of Memmius son of Asclepius, who declared he couldn't write. The latter lends his manpower in order to work in the goldmines of Aurelius Adiutor, from this day until the 15th of November, for a payment of 70 denarii and food. He will receive payment as agreed and will have to work seriously. If he was to quit without Aurelius Adiutor's approval, he will have to pay for each day 5 sestertes and 8 ases. If floods will make work impossible, everything will be calculated proportionally. If the leaseholder will be late with the payment, he will have to pay penalties as well, after three times. Sealed at Immenosum Maius.
