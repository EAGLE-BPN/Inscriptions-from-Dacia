I(ovi) O(ptimo) M(aximo)
Iunoni Regi-
nae et pro sa-
lute Imp(eratoris) L(ucii) Sep-
5timi(i) Severi Aug(usti)
et L(ucii) Sep(timii) Anto-

Translation:

To Jupiter the Good and Greatest and to Iuno the Queen and for the health of the Emperors Lucius Septimius Severus Augustus and Lucius Septimius Antoninus and of the Caesar Geta. Claudius Cocceius erected this altar on his own expenses with ten days before the beginning of July (20th of June) while Victorinus and Severus were consuls (200 AD)
