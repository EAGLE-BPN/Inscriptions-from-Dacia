Translation:

Krystallus, a child nourished by Postumus, the prefect of the fleet; his origins are where the Iberus flows, Roman by birth he was educated in the military camps and on the see; after he saw the lands of Iberia and the old Moorish fortifications, he rests in the Moesia. He died young, just like the seeds of the spring-flowers are carried by the cold Thracian wind. Achelous, a child nourished by Postumus, the prefect of the fleet; I was born in the far land of the Pontic Cappadocia, on the shore, where the walls of Tyana are; my father was Hermogenes, Achelous a skillful boy, raised on the shore by a man...


Commentary:

First twelve verses are written in diameter iambic, the next (15-20) in the elegiac couplet
