Source: UNIVERSITATEA BABES BOLYAI


Author: Rada Varga

Description

Object type: statue base
Material: limestone
Type of Inscription: votive inscription
Execution Technique: carved
Letters: yes 3-4
Provenance: Napoca
Findspot: str. Memorandumului, no. 8, Romania
Date: L. Valerius Messalla and C. Octavius Appius Suetrius Sabinus were consuls in 214(A. Degrassi, Fasti, p. 60)

Bibliography:

  • A.Buday, Dolgozatók 4, 1913, p. 255-257B
  • AE 1960, 226

Text

Deae Suriae pro saluṭ[e Imp(eratoris) Caes(aris) M(arci) Aur(elii) Antonini p(ii) f(elicis)]
Aug(usti) Pa[r]t(hici) max(imi) Brit(annici) max(imi) G[er(manici) max(imi) et Iuliae Aug(ustae) matris Aug(usti)]
et castrorum senat[us ac patriae dedicante(?) L(ucio) Mario]
Perpetuo co(n)s(ulari) Dac(iarum) IIỊ[---]
5Aureli(i) Claudi(i) Nepoṣ[---]
fratres empto[loc]o[---]
sive ab eis quos ipsi[---]
d(ono) [d(ederunt)]
Messa[la et Sabino co(n)s(ulibus)].

Translation:

To th goddess Suria for the health of the emperor CaesarMarcus Aurelius Antoninus, the pious, the happy, Augustus, great defeater of the Partians, of the Britans, of the Germans and to Iulia Augusta, mother of the emperor, of the military camps and of the Senat, Lucius Marius Perpetuu, consularis of the 3 Dacias, .... nephew of Aurelius Claudius...brothers, after having bought a property,...themselves..., when Messala and Sabinus were consuls.
