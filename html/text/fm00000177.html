Object type: Plate
Material: Marble
Type of Inscription: Votive

Date: 144-147 AD, when Ti. Claudius Saturninus was governor of the Lower Moesia province

Text

I(ovi) O(ptimo) M(aximo)
pro salute Imp(eratoris) Caes(aris) T(iti) Ael(i) Ha-
driani Antonini Aug(usti) Pii et Ve-
ri Caes(aris) templum et statuam
5c(ivibus) R(omanis) et consisstentibus (sic!) in
canabis Aelis leg(ionis) XI Cl(audiae)
Cn(aeus) Oppius Soterichus et
Oppius Severus, fil(ius) eius,
de suo fecerunt. Dedica-
10tum est per Tib(erium) Cl(audium) Saturni-
num, leg(atum) Aug(usti) pr(o) p(raetore), Tib(erio) Cl(audio) Iuliano, leg(ato) Aug(usti)

Translation:

To Jupiter the Good and the Greatest, for the good health of the Emperor Caesar Titus Aelius Hadrianus Antoninus Augustus Pius and Verus Caesar, Cnaeus Oppius Soterichus and Oppius Severus, his son, made the temple and the statue for the Roman citizen who have their residence in the canabae Aeliae of the eleventh Claudia legion. This is dedicated by Tiberius Claudius Saturninus, imperial legate of the province, and by Tiberius Claudius Iulianus, the imperial legate of the legion.
